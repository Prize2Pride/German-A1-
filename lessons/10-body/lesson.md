## 2. GRAMMAR (القواعد)

### Articles and Cases (أدوات التعريف وحالات الإعراب)

In German, every noun has a gender: masculine (der), feminine (die), or neuter (das). The article changes depending on the case (nominative, accusative, dative).

*   **Nominative (الحالة الإسمية):** The subject of the sentence.
    *   *Der Kopf* ist rund. (The head is round.)
*   **Accusative (حالة المفعول به):** The direct object of the sentence.
    *   Ich sehe *den Kopf*. (I see the head.)
*   **Dative (حالة الجر):** The indirect object of the sentence.
    *   Ich gebe *dem Kopf* einen Hut. (I give the head a hat.)

### Possessive Pronouns (ضمائر الملكية)

Possessive pronouns show ownership and agree with the gender and case of the noun they modify.

*   Mein Arm ist stark. (My arm is strong.)
*   Deine Hand ist kalt. (Your hand is cold.)
*   Sein Bein ist lang. (His leg is long.)

### Adjective Endings (نهايات الصفات)

Adjectives take different endings depending on the article and the case of the noun.

*   **With definite article:** Der große Mann. (The tall man.)
*   **With indefinite article:** Ein großer Mann. (A tall man.)
*   **With no article:** Großer Mann. (Tall man.)
